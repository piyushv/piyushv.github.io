Business Communication for Amity (Formerly Eko)

Android, iOS · 2018–19

In this case study, we delve into the detailed process of designing and enhancing key features for Eko, an internal communication and operations platform. My role as a Product Designer involved designing solutions for complex user needs and evolving business requirements for both iOS and Android apps.

1. Workflow Redesign

Objective and Scope

Transforming Eko's Workflow feature to handle complex organizational processes while maintaining simplicity and user friendliness.

Research

We started by analysing customer feedback and competitor benchmarks, which led to identifying key areas for improvement.

The Problems

  1. The original workflow design was limited to a single form, inadequate for intricate processes like performance reviews where we need to share different forms to different users.
  2. Absence of recurring and scheduling features led to inefficiencies.
  3. Users were requesting a custom title so they can add details like room number or specify the exact issue.

Users

Front line workers (43%) and knowledge workers (34%) create and send the workflows to the 23% managers or HRs. Front line workers don't have access to computers, so the design was made mobile-first.

Design

The redesign of the flow was done in three parts: Home screen, Workflow creation, and Workflow details.

Home

Redesigned the home screen to surface relevant workflows and quick actions for front line workers.

Creation

Workflow templates are made through the Admin panel. We added saved drafts and scheduled workflows to avoid duplicate workflows.

In the workflow input screen, schedule and priority were added.

Workflow Details

The most significant improvement was adding multiple stages to a workflow, which could now handle complex processes. It has two parts — details with redesigned statuses and timeline, including past and ongoing stages. The "Awaiting your action" stage is prominently placed at the bottom.

Outcome

+12% Workflow creation increase over one month across two clients
45,000 Users from a new client acquired because Workflow met their requirements
100,000 Additional licenses from an existing client

Credits & My Role

I was working as a Product Designer on iOS and Android App, along with a PM, Researcher, and getting feedback from other designers. Another UX designer did web design.

2. Voice and Video Call Redesign (Android & iOS)

The redesign aimed to address identified user issues: lack of app recognition during calls, inability to switch between call types, and adherence to platform-specific guidelines.

Problems & Objectives

  1. Brand Recognition: Users were unable to identify Eko as the source of incoming calls.
  2. Functionality Limitation: The inability to switch between audio and video calls during a conversation was a significant limitation.
  3. Platform Compliance: The need to integrate Apple's CallKit for iOS to align with Human Interface Guidelines.

Old Design

All screens were collected to visualise the existing flow and identify improvements.

New Design

  1. Brand Integration: We incorporated the Eko logo and a clear "Eko call" notification on the incoming call screen so users immediately recognized the source of the call.
  2. Enhanced Call Flexibility: Added a button to seamlessly switch between audio and video modes.
  3. Platform-Specific Adjustments: For iOS, a Gaussian blur background was introduced, aligning with iOS aesthetics. For Android, a dark background was implemented for a comfortable viewing experience.
  4. UI Enhancements: A dedicated bar in the Recents page allowed users to easily return to ongoing calls. An indicator on user avatars was added to signify active calls.

3. Reply-to Feature (Android & iOS)

This section outlines the design process and implementation of the "Reply-to" feature in Eko's chat application for both Android and iOS platforms.

Process

We started by identifying all the types of messages in our app: text, voice, image, video, file and sticker. To ensure our solution was competitive, we conducted an in-depth analysis of similar features in other leading apps.

The project had a quick turnaround time of 2 weeks.

Design

The central challenge was to create a user interface that seamlessly accommodates replies to diverse message types. The design needed to be intuitive, minimizing user effort while maintaining clarity in threaded conversations.
