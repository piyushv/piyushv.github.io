The Opportunity

Post-pandemic, people started living in places instead of visiting them. Remote work went mainstream, bleisure travel surged, and Agoda spotted a growth opportunity in long-stay accommodations.

A survey run by our researcher confirmed the demand. Five key drivers stood out: remote work flexibility, slower travel, change of scenery, affordability of weekly/monthly discounts, and convenience of hotel amenities.

Competitive Desk Research

I looked at how Airbnb, Booking.com, and other OTAs handled long-stay bookings. Every platform assumed users already knew they wanted a long stay. Nobody was converting short-stay browsers into long-stay bookers within the booking flow.

That was our gap, intervene during the existing funnel to surface value.

The Problem

Agoda's booking flow treated a 1-night stay and a 30-night stay identically. Users couldn't discover long-stay discounts, compare pricing across stay lengths, or feel confident they were getting the best deal.

Design challenge: Increase long-stay bookings by making discounts discoverable, comparisons effortless, and the value clear. Across Android, iOS, and web.

Understanding Long-Stay Users

Segment Typical stay Key need
Remote workers / digital nomads 2 to 8 weeks Reliable wifi, workspace, home-like setup
Budget travelers 1 to 4 weeks Lowest possible nightly rate
Travelers seeking a change of scenery 1 to 3 months New environments, exploration
Extended vacationers 1 to 2 weeks Relaxation, amenities, flexibility

Each group responds to different signals. Budget travelers react to discount badges immediately. Extended vacationers need a nudge at the right moment. Remote workers want to compare numbers and feel confident in the value.

Jobs to Be Done

Working with our researcher, I mapped user needs through JTBD:

  1. "Help me find a long-stay deal without searching for hours." Discounted rates needed to be surfaced, not buried in price breakdowns.
  2. "Let me compare: is 7 nights actually cheaper per night than 5?" The math wasn't obvious. Users needed visual clarity.
  3. "Make me confident I'm not overpaying." Pricing transparency matters for high-value bookings.
  4. "Let me extend my stay without starting over." Users already on a trip wanted a frictionless way to add nights.

Strategy: Awareness → Nudge → Education

I proposed a phased rollout. If Phase 1 badges didn't move bookings, we'd rethink Phase 2. This de-risked engineering effort and gave us data to justify continued investment.

  1. Awareness. Help users discover that long-stay discounts exist.
  2. Nudge. Motivate users near a discount threshold to extend their stay.
  3. Education. Help users understand that discounts grow with stay length. Build confidence. Reduce cancellations.

The Touchpoint System I Designed

A connected system of interventions across the booking funnel, each triggering at specific moments and stay-length thresholds:

Touchpoint Where When it triggers
Homepage banner Homepage User hasn't selected dates. General awareness.
Hotel card badge Search results Hotel offers long-stay discount AND user selected 5, 6, 7, 12, 13, 14, 26, 27, or 28 days
Nudge banner Search results, property page User is 1 to 2 days before a threshold. Shows savings, "Learn more" CTA.
Bottom sheet Overlays current page Triggered by "Learn more." Full discount breakdown.
Education banner Property page Shows discounted price for the selected stay length.
Discount in price breakdown Price breakdown Discount name and amount as a line item.
Discount in checkout Checkout Reinforces savings at the commitment moment.
Calendar dotted line Date picker User selects 7, 14, or 28 days. Dotted line extends to show what 1 to 2 extra days would look like.

The targeting logic made this work. Three threshold clusters where users were close to unlocking a discount tier:

  • 5, 6, 7 days → weekly discount threshold
  • 12, 13, 14 days → bi-weekly discount threshold
  • 26, 27, 28 days → monthly discount threshold

The behavioral gap is 1 to 2 extra days. The financial benefit is concrete. These users had the highest conversion potential.

Phase 1: Awareness

Hypothesis: Users aren't booking long-stays because they don't know discounts exist.

What I designed: "Weekly discount" badges at three decision points. Search results (where users scan), property details (where users evaluate), and price breakdown (where users commit).

Design decisions:

The badge sits next to the price, as on Agoda's dense search results, users scan price first. Which means zero extra effort to notice it.

The badge is compact and uses a Red color to stand out without adding noise.

Adapted sizing, spacing, and typography across web, Android, and iOS to feel native on each platform.

Price breakdown: discount as a line item

The discount appears as a named line item. "Weekly discount" or "Monthly discount" with the exact amount. For high-value bookings, users scrutinize the price breakdown. A named, visible discount reinforces the deal.

Checkout: final reinforcement

The discount carries through to checkout. At the commitment moment, seeing the savings one more time provides reassurance.

Result: +10% increase in long-stay bookings

Phase 2: Nudge

Hypothesis: Users near a discount threshold will extend their stay if you show them exact savings.

What I designed: Two banner types, a bottom sheet, and a calendar enhancement.

Contextual nudge banner (threshold users)

On search results and property pages, users who selected dates 1 or 2 days before a threshold (6 days, 13 days, 27 days) see a targeted nudge. It shows the specific savings for adding 1 to 2 more days. And offers a "Learn more" CTA that opens a bottom sheet with the full discount breakdown plus the option to change dates.

Calendar dotted line

When a user selects exactly 7, 14, or 28 days, a dotted line extends 1 to 2 days beyond their selection. A visual hint that extending slightly would unlock savings.

The user sees their solid selection and a ghost of what could be. No dates changed. No flow interrupted. Just the threshold made visible at the moment of decision.

Result: Average length of stay increased by 2.4 days

Users weren't just adding 1 night. They rethought their trip duration entirely, often jumping to the next discount tier.

Phase 3: Education

Hypothesis: If users see the discount reinforced at every step, they'll feel more confident and cancel less.

Homepage banner (no dates selected)

Users landing without dates see a general long-stay awareness banner. Plants the idea that discounts exist before they start searching.

The bottom sheet behind "Learn more" was used instead of showing everything inline as the nudge banner has to be compact enough to sit within search results without disrupting scanning. And interested users can get more information if needed.

Property page: education banner

Shows the actual discounted price for the selected stay length. Users see the concrete value they're getting.

Why this reduces cancellations: Users see the discount at multiple points. The perceived cost of canceling goes up. They've locked in a deal. Walking away means losing specific, quantified savings.

Result: Decrease in cancellation rate (add specific %)

How the Phases Connected

Three phases, one funnel of progressive commitment:

Phase 1 badges answered: "Does this hotel offer a long-stay discount?"

Phase 2 nudge banners answered: "How much would I save by staying longer?"

Phase 3 education banners answered: "Am I getting a good deal?"

A user could hit all three in one session. See the badge on search results, notice they're 1 day short of a discount, tap "Learn more" for the full breakdown, adjust dates, then see the discount in price breakdown and checkout. Each touchpoint reinforced the last.

Cumulative Results

What I Learned

  • Surfacing existing value was the highest leverage move. The discounts already existed. Users couldn't find them. The entire project was about visibility and timing.
  • Threshold targeting expanded the addressable audience. Three clusters (5 to 7, 12 to 14, 26 to 28 days) covered far more users than a single threshold. The 2.4-day average increase suggests many users jumped past the nearest threshold to the next one.
  • The calendar dotted line punched above its weight. Subtlest intervention, outsized impact. It appeared at the exact moment of date selection, the highest-intent interaction in the flow.
  • Phased rollout built internal confidence. Phase 1's +10% lift made it easy to get buy-in for Phase 2. Each phase's data justified the next.

What I'd do differently:

Personalized thresholds. With more time, I'd explore nudges based on booking history, destination, and traveler segment.

Expand threshold logic to more stay-length ranges.
